AI-Powered Diagnostic-Aware Medical Image Compression

What This Website Does

A diagnostic-aware compression framework that redefines what it means to compress medical images. Instead of optimizing for visual quality, it optimizes for diagnostic preservation — explicitly learning which features are crucial for disease classification and allocating bits accordingly.

Why It's Important

Every day, hospitals around the world generate massive volumes of medical imaging data — from radiology scans to digital pathology slides. A single CT scan can be hundreds of megabytes, and large health systems can accumulate tens of petabytes each year. Storing and transferring this flood of data costs healthcare providers tens of millions of dollars annually and slows down remote diagnosis and technology-supported care.

Existing image compression methods — like JPEG or even learned codecs — are built for how images look to humans, not for how well they preserve what actually matters: diagnostic information. When these algorithms compress too aggressively, they erase subtle features that AI models rely on to detect diseases like cancer or pneumonia, compromising clinical reliability.
